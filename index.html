the symbolism of hydrangeas

the etymological meaning of hydrangea stems from the greek words for water, hydros and jar, angos.

in japan, they are said to be a sign of apology or gratitude because an emperor gave them as apologies to his maidens. contrastingly, hydrangeas have a negative sentiment in europe where they were used to declare arrogance. they also became synonymous with frigidity in the medieval ages because it was believed that young women who grew them would never find husbands.

the history of hydrangeas

the hydrangea was first cultivated in japan, but ancient hydrangea fossils dating back to 40-65 million years ago have been discovered in north america. hydrangeas didnâ€™t appear in europe until 1736 when a colonist brought a north american varietal to england.

hydrangeas are primarily used for landscaping because their petals contain low levels of cyanide, making them unfit for consumption. the exception is hydrangea serrata which buddhists drink in a sweet tea as part of a cleansing ritual. the tea is said to help treat autoimmune disorders as well as malaria, kidney stones and enlarged prostate.

in western culture the hydrangea has many different uses. native americans used the root as a diuretic and the bark as pain relief specifically for muscle pain and burns. traditionally, hydrangeas are also a polite way of telling a suitor you are uninterested. now they are beautiful garden decoration that line many houses in suburban america.

color meaning

blue: connected to frigidity, turning down a romantic proposal, asking for forgiveness, and expressing regret

white: symbolizes arrogance, vanity or boasting, as well as purity and grace

pink: symbolize heartfelt feelings, sincere emotions and the meaning of love

purple: represents the desire for deep understanding and appreciation for your partner. symbolizes abundance and wealth
